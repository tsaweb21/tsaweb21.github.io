Our Background

The idea of Avalon was created in a dorm room at the University of California, Berkeley. Its two developers sought to find something in common in the world they were navigating. Sebastian Graham, an environmental engineer and his roommate, Cameron Richards, a mechanical engineer were placed as roommates for the year and struggled with many disagreements. In all the tension and arguing, the two had approached a topic that would change it all; it was on the rise, and both were fiery with opinions about it. Electric cars. Attempting to find a mediator, Milani Mou, an environmental engineer studying for her masters degree was roped into the debate. Through an argument that took many days and notebooks that where to be filled in the process, Avalon had its beginning. It was a car company with a sole purpose not to simply make a profit, but to rather change the industry of transportation as a whole. Its creators tried to make the forms of it more reusable and the longevity of the design increase, with a feeling of great luxury. With this, the beginning stages of Avalon began to come together. Years of researching and countless long nights have brought us to now, with a company that shows endless promise for the future of automobiles.
